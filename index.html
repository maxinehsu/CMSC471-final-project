Outbreak Timeline

This view compares measles vaccination coverage (MCV1) against reported case counts for a selected country over time. Use the country dropdown to switch locations, then drag the brush at the bottom to zoom into a specific date range. Hover anywhere in the chart to inspect the nearest year: the tooltip and highlight markers show the exact coverage level, case count, and whether that year is above or below the herd-immunity threshold.

Click to see how data is represented on the graph

Cases are shown on a logarithmic scale because outbreaks can vary by orders of magnitude, from small baseline levels to very large spikes. A linear scale would flatten smaller values and hide meaningful changes, while the log scale keeps both low and high periods readable in the same frame. The dashed 95% reference line reflects a commonly used measles herd-immunity benchmark: when coverage drops below this level, population protection weakens and outbreak risk rises, which is why those segments are explicitly highlighted as a danger zone.

Global Outbreak Map

This map combines two signals for the selected disease and year: country color encodes vaccination coverage, while bubble size encodes reported case counts. Use the disease dropdown to switch between measles, diphtheria/pertussis, and polio, then move the year slider (or press "Play") to animate how risk changes over time. Hover over a country or bubble to highlight it, inspect exact values in the tooltip, and compare how local coverage and outbreak burden shift across regions.
